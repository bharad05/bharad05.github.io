About

Hi, I am Bharadwaj Vangipuram, a PhD student in Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of California, Davis, advised by Prof. Alvar Escriva-Bou. I bridge systems engineering, optimization modeling, and risk communication to address hydrologic hazards, with a particular focus on translating complex hydrologic and economic datasets into clear, actionable insights for policymakers and vulnerable communities.

Before joining UC Davis, I was a Graduate Research Assistant at the Centre for Urban Sustainability & Resilience at University College London, where I developed mathematical optimization models for water quality trading systems. I also worked as a United Nations Environmental Researcher with the Morobe Development Foundation in Papua New Guinea, synthesising watershed risk evidence for the governments of Australia and Papua New Guinea.

My current work contributes to California's 5th Climate Change Assessment, characterising drought risk and uncertainty across multi-decadal agricultural and urban datasets to support state-level climate adaptation planning.

Projects

California 5th Climate Change Assessment

This project focuses on characterising drought risk and uncertainty for California's state-level climate adaptation planning. Working as a Graduate Student Researcher at UC Davis, I conduct data-driven analysis of multi-decadal agricultural and urban datasets, translate hydro-economic modeling results into evidence-based policy narratives, and develop data visualizations to communicate complex water demand trade-offs and climate risks to non-technical policy audiences.




Water Quality Trading as a Risk Management Instrument

This project developed a mathematical optimization model in GAMS to simulate nutrient loading and evaluate water quality trading as a risk management instrument. I coupled hydrologic constraints with economic variables to assess the effectiveness of market-based mechanisms in reducing nitrogen concentrations under alternative regulatory scenarios, visualizing and interpreting model outputs to communicate environmental risk–tradeoff dynamics to researchers and policy-relevant audiences. This work produced three peer-reviewed publications in Water Research, ACS ES&T Engineering, and the Journal of Environmental Management.




Nutrient Neutrality & Urban Development Trade-offs

This project integrated housing demand forecasts with nutrient risk constraints to model development scenarios affecting a population of approximately 120,000. I analysed time-series environmental data (~700 observations) to quantify trade-offs between urban growth, regulatory compliance, and water quality protection, contributing to market-based approaches to nutrient neutrality policy in the UK.




Bumbu Watershed Water Quality — Papua New Guinea

Working with the Morobe Development Foundation under a UNEP-affiliated mandate, I collaborated with international partners to interpret water quality monitoring data and evaluate phytoremediation options for watershed-scale risk mitigation. Findings were synthesised into a policy-oriented report funded by the Governments of Australia and Papua New Guinea.
