About

I am a PhD researcher at UC Davis working at the intersection of water systems, hydrologic risk, and climate risk finance. My work translates complex drought and water-scarcity data into actionable insights for policymakers, insurers, and vulnerable communities — bridging the gap between physical hydrology and real-world financial decision-making.

Work

Projects

California 5th Climate Change Assessment

This project focuses on characterising drought risk and uncertainty for California's state-level climate adaptation planning. Working as a Graduate Student Researcher at UC Davis, I conduct data-driven analysis of multi-decadal agricultural and urban datasets, translate hydro-economic modeling results into evidence-based policy narratives, and develop data visualizations to communicate complex water demand trade-offs and climate risks to non-technical policy audiences.

Water Quality Trading as a Risk Management Instrument

This project developed a mathematical optimization model in GAMS to simulate nutrient loading and evaluate water quality trading as a risk management instrument. I coupled hydrologic constraints with economic variables to assess the effectiveness of market-based mechanisms in reducing nitrogen concentrations under alternative regulatory scenarios. This work produced three peer-reviewed publications in Water Research, ACS ES&T Engineering, and the Journal of Environmental Management.

Nutrient Neutrality & Urban Development Trade-offs

This project integrated housing demand forecasts with nutrient risk constraints to model development scenarios affecting a population of approximately 120,000. I analysed time-series environmental data (~700 observations) to quantify trade-offs between urban growth, regulatory compliance, and water quality protection.

Bumbu Watershed Water Quality — Papua New Guinea

Working with the Morobe Development Foundation under a UNEP-affiliated mandate, I collaborated with international partners to interpret water quality monitoring data and evaluate phytoremediation options for watershed-scale risk mitigation. Findings were synthesised into a policy-oriented report funded by the Governments of Australia and Papua New Guinea.
