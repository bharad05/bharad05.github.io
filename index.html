About

I am a PhD student in Civil & Environmental Engineering at UC Davis, advised by Prof. Alvar Escriva-Bou. My research sits at the intersection of hydrology, systems engineering, and climate risk finance — translating complex hydrologic and economic datasets into actionable insights for policymakers and vulnerable communities.

Before UC Davis I was a Graduate Research Assistant at the Centre for Urban Sustainability & Resilience, University College London, where I developed optimization models for water quality trading systems. Earlier I worked as a United Nations Environmental Researcher with the Morobe Development Foundation in Papua New Guinea, synthesising watershed risk evidence for the governments of Australia and PNG.

My current work contributes to California's 5th Climate Change Assessment, focusing on drought risk characterisation and climate adaptation decision support.

Research

UC Davis · 2025–Present

California 5th Climate Change Assessment

Characterising drought risk and uncertainty across multi-decadal agricultural and urban datasets for California's state-level climate adaptation planning. Translating hydro-economic modeling results into policy-relevant narratives and data visualisations for non-technical stakeholder audiences.

UCL · 2022–2024

Water Quality Trading as a Risk Management Instrument

Developed a mathematical optimisation model in GAMS to simulate nutrient loading and evaluate water quality trading under alternative regulatory scenarios. Coupled hydrologic constraints with economic variables to assess market-based mechanisms for reducing nitrogen concentrations in river systems.

UCL · 2022–2024

Nutrient Neutrality & Urban Development Trade-offs

Integrated housing demand forecasts with nutrient risk constraints to model development scenarios for a population of ~120,000. Analysed ~700 observations of time-series environmental data to quantify trade-offs between urban growth, regulatory compliance, and water quality protection.

UNEP / Morobe Development Foundation · 2019–2021

Bumbu Watershed Water Quality — Papua New Guinea

Collaborated with international partners to interpret water quality monitoring data and evaluate phytoremediation options for watershed-scale risk mitigation. Synthesised hydrologic risk evidence into policy-oriented findings for a report funded by the Governments of Australia and Papua New Guinea.
